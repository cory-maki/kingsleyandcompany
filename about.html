Our Story

Building a Different Kind of Real Estate Firm

Kingsley Consulting, doing business as Kingsley + Co., was built on a simple idea: connect the right people, capital, and expertise, and even the most overlooked neighborhood can be worth investing in. Chinedum Ndukwe founded the firm to bring that discipline to Cincinnati, forming strategic partnerships and developing business solutions designed to revitalize communities rather than simply extract value from them.

That founding mission still shapes every deal Kingsley + Co. takes on. Ndukwe and his team look for sites and neighborhoods that conventional capital tends to pass over, and they build the partnerships needed to make investment there make sense.

Our Approach

Full-Spectrum Real Estate Expertise

From site acquisition to asset management, Kingsley + Co. stretches across the full spectrum of real estate and development. The firm specializes in identifying lucrative opportunities and assembling teams of players to invest in and execute projects through stabilization and beyond.

Kingsley + Co.'s offerings include redevelopment, asset management, leasing, design & build, acquisitions & disposition, investment, financing & diverse capital, and sustainability — giving partners a single firm to work with from the first site visit through long-term ownership.

Our Focus

Investing Where It's Needed Most

A recurring thread across Kingsley + Co.'s work is affordable housing in low-income communities. The firm's senior housing project in Walnut Hills is a direct expression of that focus: bringing new, affordable units to elderly residents in a neighborhood that had gone without meaningful new investment for years.

Chinedum Ndukwe has been direct about why this matters to him — real estate development should leave a community better off than it found it, not just deliver a return to investors. That philosophy guides how Kingsley + Co. selects, structures, and executes every project.

Leadership

Led By Chinedum Ndukwe

Before founding Kingsley + Co., Chinedum Ndukwe built a career defined by discipline and teamwork as a professional football player. He's carried both into real estate, assembling the partnerships and capital stacks that turn a promising site into a finished, occupied project.

Ndukwe stays closely involved with the communities Kingsley + Co. builds in, showing up at groundbreakings, ribbon cuttings, and neighborhood events — a habit reflected in his updates on LinkedIn and the firm's project photos on Instagram.

Recognition

Recognized Growth, Real Impact

In 2025, the Cincinnati Business Courier named Kingsley + Co. a Power 25 Growth Champion, recognizing the firm among the region's fastest-growing minority-owned businesses. It's a marker of momentum, but Ndukwe measures the firm's progress just as much by the neighborhoods it's helped rebuild — including the student housing development near the University of Cincinnati that opened new options for residents in Corryville.

You can follow the firm's ongoing projects and community work on its Facebook page and browse groundbreaking footage on its YouTube channel.
