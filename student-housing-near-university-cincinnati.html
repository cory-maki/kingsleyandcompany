New Student Housing Rises Near the University of Cincinnati

📅 August 14, 2025
✎ Kingsley + Co.
⌚ 4 min read

Kingsley + Co. has unveiled a new student housing development in Corryville, adding apartment units within walking distance of the University of Cincinnati in a submarket that has struggled to keep pace with demand.

The Corryville project marks another entry in Kingsley + Co.'s growing portfolio of Cincinnati redevelopment work, following close behind the firm's senior housing groundbreaking in Walnut Hills. Where that project targets elderly residents on fixed incomes, the Corryville development addresses a different but equally pressing gap: quality, well-located housing for the university's student population.

Filling a Gap Near Campus

Neighborhoods surrounding the University of Cincinnati, including Corryville and the broader Euclid corridor, have seen steady enrollment growth outpace new housing supply in recent years. Kingsley + Co. identified the site as an opportunity to redevelop underused property into apartment housing built specifically for students and young professionals working near campus.

Chinedum Ndukwe said the project reflects the same acquisition-through-management approach the firm applies across its portfolio: identify a site with real, demonstrated demand, assemble the capital and design team, and hold the asset for the long term rather than flipping it after construction.

"We look for sites where the need is obvious and the execution is hard — that's where Kingsley + Co. adds the most value." — Chinedum Ndukwe

Design & Build, Start to Finish

The Corryville development leaned on Kingsley + Co.'s in-house design & build capability, one of the eight core service lines described on the firm's about page. Keeping design, construction, and leasing under one team allowed the firm to move from acquisition to occupancy on a tighter timeline than a typical multi-vendor build.

The project also builds on the momentum that helped earn Kingsley + Co. recognition as a Power 25 Growth Champion earlier in 2025, adding a second major groundbreaking to the firm's year.

What's Next for the Corridor

Kingsley + Co. says it continues to evaluate additional sites near the university corridor as part of a broader push to add housing supply in neighborhoods adjacent to Cincinnati's core. Updates on leasing timelines and future phases will be shared through the firm's Instagram account.

Conclusion

The Corryville development is the latest example of Kingsley + Co. applying its full-spectrum model — acquisition, design & build, and long-term management — to a Cincinnati neighborhood with clear, unmet housing demand.
