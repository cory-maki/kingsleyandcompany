Kingsley + Co. Breaks Ground on Walnut Hills Senior Housing

📅 March 25, 2025
✎ Kingsley + Co.
⌚ 4 min read

Kingsley + Co. has broken ground on a new affordable senior housing development along the Reading Road corridor in Walnut Hills, a project the firm has called one of the most urgently needed in its portfolio.

The development, known as Victory Vistas, will bring new senior housing units to a Cincinnati neighborhood that has gone years without significant new residential investment. Chinedum Ndukwe, principal of Kingsley + Co., described the project as a direct answer to the mission that led him to found the firm: connecting partners and capital to communities that conventional development tends to pass over.

Why Walnut Hills, Why Now

Walnut Hills has a large population of longtime elderly residents, many on fixed incomes, who have limited housing options as the surrounding market shifts. Kingsley + Co. structured the Victory Vistas project specifically to serve that population, prioritizing affordability alongside quality construction and long-term asset management.

"This is exactly the kind of project Kingsley + Co. exists to build," Ndukwe said at the groundbreaking. "Real estate development should leave a neighborhood better off than it found it."

"Real estate development should leave a neighborhood better off than it found it." — Chinedum Ndukwe

Assembling the Partnership

Bringing an affordable senior housing project from concept to groundbreaking requires the kind of full-spectrum capability Kingsley + Co. was built around — site acquisition, financing and diverse capital, design & build, and long-term asset management, all coordinated under one team. That approach is detailed further on the firm's about page.

Kingsley + Co. worked with local partners and public financing sources to structure a deal that pencils out for a low-income senior population, a task that requires patient capital and a long-term view of asset management rather than a quick exit.

What Comes Next

Construction on Victory Vistas is underway, with Kingsley + Co. continuing to manage the asset through stabilization and beyond. The project sits alongside other recent Kingsley + Co. work, including a student housing development near the University of Cincinnati, as part of the firm's broader push to add housing across Cincinnati neighborhoods with real need.

Kingsley + Co. expects to share updates on construction milestones and leasing as the project progresses toward completion.

Conclusion

The Walnut Hills groundbreaking reflects the throughline in Kingsley + Co.'s work: identifying communities with real housing need and assembling the capital and partnerships to meet it. It's the same focus that earned the firm recognition as a Power 25 Growth Champion in 2025.
